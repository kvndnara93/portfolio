Context The target for Q4 2025–Q1 2026 was to grow paid media investment by ~30% without letting blended ROAS slip below 5×. Scaling spend is the easy part — protecting efficiency while you do it is the hard part.

What I did Grew budgets market-by-market, but only where returns held — reallocating spend continuously toward the highest-ROAS campaigns and channels rather than lifting every budget uniformly, and leaning on my own AI recommendations layer to catch efficiency drift before it showed up in the monthly numbers.

Result Delivered ~35% spend growth while lifting blended ROAS from 4.8× to over 6.5× — ahead of plan on both the growth and the efficiency target.

Context The flagship UAE account was running a fragmented mix of legacy campaigns — a feed-ROAS campaign, tactical retargeting and a capped DPA campaign — all chasing the same shoppers and splitting the conversion signal three ways. My read: consolidating them into Advantage+ would let Meta pool that signal and lift ROAS without spending more.

What I changed Wound down the legacy campaigns and rebuilt the account around Advantage+ Shopping / DPA (EN + AR) — one broad, signal-rich structure instead of three partial ones. I proved it on UAE first with budget held essentially flat, so any gain came from structure, not spend.

Result Over the three months either side of the migration, ROAS jumped ~4.9× → ~8.2× (peaking 8.8×) and CPA roughly halved — on only ~18% more budget — while monthly purchases doubled:

Context On non-brand Search, broad match unlocks reach and lets Smart Bidding discover new converting queries — but left unmanaged it quietly burns budget on irrelevant searches and drags Quality Score down. The goal was to run broad match as the growth engine of non-brand Search without losing efficiency.

What I did Built the non-brand "Generic Terms" prospecting campaigns on broad match, steered by target-ROAS bidding (Maximize Conversion Value at a 400% target) so Google only buys reach where it can hit the return threshold. I made that safe by enabling Enhanced Conversions for Web — sending richer, hashed first-party data from each conversion back to Google Ads via GTM — so Smart Bidding is fed more complete, higher-quality conversion data. Then I keep the reach clean with an extensive, continuously-pruned negative-keyword system: 15 curated shared lists totalling ~5,900 negatives, reviewed weekly and layered across every campaign so junk queries are excluded before they spend.

Result Over the Mar–Jul window, the broad-match keywords in the non-brand Generic Terms campaign returned a 5.57× ROAS at a 2.48% conversion rate, while holding Quality Scores of 7–10 and mostly "above average" ad relevance — comfortably clearing the 400% (4×) floor the tROAS strategy is set to. Broad reach, without the efficiency or Quality-Score erosion it usually gets blamed for; the wider EN Search account (brand + non-brand) runs around 7× blended.

Context GA4 is the default source of truth most marketers optimise against — but platform analytics always misses some share of real transactions.

What I did Built a reconciliation report comparing GA4-tracked purchases directly against actual ERP order records across web, marketplace and in-store channels, rather than trusting GA4's number in isolation.
